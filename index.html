Tequity was founded as a response to several unaddressed needs at UC Berkeley. First, there is a lack of community for minorities in tech at UC Berkeley. Not identifying with or looking like the majority of classmates in tech can be an isolating experience.. Secondly, there is little space for communication between minority students and their allies. Existing diversity-promoting clubs cater to those who identify into one specific minority group. Lastly, there is a need for conversations between minority students in tech and the industries trying to include them. The lack of diversity in the technology industry has been well documented, and is widely acknowledged.

In response to these needs, Tequity will function as a bridge among minority students, between minority students and their allies, and between students and industry professionals.
